Vranec Luda Mara

Vranec Luda Mara offers us a deep Bordeaux flirty nose of blackcurrant, plum, blueberry and cedar wood. The taste is full and big with fresh acidity and a refreshing character that has the attributes of a great wine. Balance between acidity and dark fruitiness, where good barrel handling is the key, gives the wine a nice vitality. Concentrated dark berry nose, hints of cedar and leather. Tight, dense, with stable tannins and fresh fruitiness.

Château L’Hospitalet La Réserve

This super wine is a real fallow find made from the well-known trinity: syrah, grenache and mourverde. Phenomenal mammoth with the taste of cocoa, spices, raw begar, lavender and mocca. Gérard Bertrand leads the way for organic, biodynamic and sustainable viticulture in the south of France.

Lalume

Superbly good and well-rounded from the not quite common grape treixadura, Lalume is an aromatic white wine with floral and white fruit notes, and hints of aromatic herbs. It's luscious, full-bodied and inviting, with taste of yellow apples, pineapple and a perfectly balanced acidity. Just as good with fish as with chicken.

Ramos Reserva

Ramos Reserva is a trio of Portuguese grapes where aragonêz (also known as tempranillo) plays the main role. The taste is so berry delicious with hints of blueberries, blackberries, plums, with a pinch of licorice and a shower of violet tablets. Reserva is a sweet, but dry wine that goes happily with the late summer barbecue or with a plum-studded pork loin with mushroom sauce and hazelnut potatoes.

Stoneleigh Latitude

Stoneleigh Latitude is a chardonnay from New Zealand with notes of melon, browned butter, roasted nuts and vanilla and an aftertaste that is warming and long. If you have slightly stronger cheeses, it can be good to have a little pull under the galoshes when it comes to the wine. Then you want a little barrel character, a little butteriness, a little spice and a little sun-ripened. In this New Zealand treat you will find all that.

Vel'Uveyra Mencía

Vel'uveyra, which in the old Galician vernacular means "to gaze at the vineyards" represents the nuances of the grape harvest in this beautiful Mencia. Super stylish with lots of concentration and exciting flavor layers created from dark berries and some freshly ground black pepper, topped with a hint of violet and some iron. Excellent for classic entrecote or venison.
